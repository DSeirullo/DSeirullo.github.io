Foundations and Connections on Composite Vessels
  • Introduction into Composites — extensive literature review on composite materials used by Damen Workboats, covering definitions, the three main types and their properties, design methodology and constraints, and manufacturing processes.
  • Connection methods — overview of available connection methods to composite, their application, pros and cons.
  • Foundation design framework — a framework for the design of foundations on composite work vessels.
  • Calculation tool — an automated calculation tool for use in complex load case analyses.
Abstract: Damen Workboats develops a range of standardised vessels for a variety of applications. Due to the high degree of standardisation, the delivery time of vessels is kept to a minimum. However, the equipment and interior can vary depending on the application and client. This means that currently, every foundation – that is the structure used to support objects such as cabinets, generators, and even the engines – needs to be designed independently. The purpose of this study is to optimise the design and implementation of foundations and connections for static, non-structural objects on the composite structures of workboats and to develop a workflow/set of design principles that assists engineers in selecting the appropriate type of foundation and/or connection based on specific situational inputs – with the aim of increasing standardisation and reducing production time, weight, and/or costs of composite vessels.
